Nilakantha Somayaji

Born: 1444 CE, Kerala, India

Died: 1544 CE

Profession: Mathematician and Astronomer

School: Kerala School of Astronomy and Mathematics

Known For: Contributions to trigonometry, calculus, and astronomy

Contributions

  • Authored the famous treatise Tantrasamgraha, a major work in Indian astronomy and mathematics.
  • Presented an early version of the infinite series expansion for sine and cosine functions, anticipating parts of modern calculus.
  • Improved planetary models based on earlier works like those of Aryabhata, incorporating corrections for Mercury and Venus.
  • Developed an improved version of the planetary model aligning with observational astronomy.

Legacy

  • Major contributor to the achievements of the Kerala School, which predated similar developments in Europe.
  • Influenced later mathematical thinking, especially in infinite series and trigonometry.
  • Remembered as one of India's greatest pre-modern scientists.
