Srinivasa Ramanujan

Born: December 22, 1887, Erode, Tamil Nadu, India

Died: April 26, 1920 (aged 32), Kumbakonam, Tamil Nadu, India

Profession: Mathematician

Known For: Extraordinary contributions to number theory, infinite series, continued fractions, and modular forms

Contributions

  • Developed over 3,900 mathematical identities and theorems, many of which were original and highly advanced.
  • Famous for Ramanujan’s Tau Function, Mock Theta Functions, and highly convergent series for π (pi).
  • Collaborated with British mathematician G.H. Hardy at Cambridge University.
  • Made pioneering discoveries in number theory, partitions, and continued fractions.

Legacy

  • Regarded as one of the greatest self-taught mathematical geniuses.
  • India observes his birthday (December 22) as National Mathematics Day.
  • His notebooks continue to inspire and influence modern mathematical research.
  • Subject of the movie The Man Who Knew Infinity (2015).
