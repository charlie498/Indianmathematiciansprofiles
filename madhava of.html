Madhava of Sangamagrama

Born: Around 1350 CE, Sangamagrama (modern-day Irinjalakuda, Kerala, India)

Died: Around 1425 CE

Profession: Mathematician and Astronomer

Known For: Founding the Kerala School of Astronomy and Mathematics

Contributions

  • Developed infinite series expansions for trigonometric functions such as sine, cosine, and arctangent.
  • Pioneered concepts of calculus centuries before Newton and Leibniz.
  • Accurately calculated the value of π (pi) using a series now known as the Madhava-Leibniz series.
  • Laid the foundation for mathematical analysis in India.
  • Wrote influential works, including the now-lost *Yuktibhāṣā* (known through later texts).

Legacy

  • Regarded as the founder of mathematical analysis in Indian mathematics.
  • Inspired generations of scholars in the Kerala school like Nilakantha Somayaji and Jyesthadeva.
  • Recognized posthumously for anticipating elements of calculus well before it emerged in Europe.
  • His work bridged pure mathematics with practical astronomy.
