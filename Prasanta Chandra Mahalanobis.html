Prasanta Chandra Mahalanobis

Born: June 29, 1893, Kolkata, India

Died: June 28, 1972, Kolkata, India

Profession: Statistician, Economist, Scientist

Known For: Mahalanobis distance, founding Indian statistical systems

Contributions

  • Introduced the Mahalanobis distance, a statistical measure widely used in multivariate analysis.
  • Founded the Indian Statistical Institute (ISI) in 1931.
  • Played a key role in large-scale surveys, including the first Indian National Sample Survey.
  • Instrumental in the design and implementation of India's Second Five-Year Plan, focusing on industrialization.

Awards and Recognition

  • Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS)
  • Padma Vibhushan (1968)
  • Member of the Planning Commission of India

Legacy

  • Father of modern statistics in India.
  • His birthday, June 29, is celebrated as National Statistics Day in India.
  • Laid the foundation for statistical planning in Indian governance and economics.
