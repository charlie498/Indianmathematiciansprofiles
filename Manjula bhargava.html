Manjul Bhargava

Born: August 8, 1974, Hamilton, Ontario, Canada

Nationality: American (of Indian descent)

Profession: Mathematician, Professor at Princeton University

Field: Number Theory

Contributions

  • Made groundbreaking work in number theory, particularly in algebraic number fields and elliptic curves.
  • Extended Gauss's composition law and developed new techniques in arithmetic geometry.
  • Introduced Bhargava cubes and several generalizations of classical mathematical structures.
  • Known for elegant, original methods often inspired by Sanskrit poetry and classical Indian mathematics.
