Brahmagupta

Born: 598 CE, Ujjain, India

Died: Around 668 CE

Profession: Mathematician and Astronomer

Famous Work: Brahmasphuṭasiddhānta (628 CE)

Contributions

  • First to define zero as a number and explain rules for arithmetic with zero.
  • Gave rules for operations on negative numbers.
  • Developed methods for solving quadratic and linear equations.
  • Explained gravity conceptually, stating objects fall to Earth due to its nature.
  • Described methods to calculate areas and volumes of geometric figures.
  • Contributed to astronomical models, including lunar and solar eclipses.

Legacy

  • Laid foundational work for number systems, especially involving zero.
  • Influenced Islamic and later European mathematics.
  • Honored as one of the most influential scholars in Indian mathematics.
