Harish-Chandra

Full Name: Harish-Chandra Mehrotra

Born: October 11, 1923, Kanpur, British India

Died: October 16, 1983, Princeton, New Jersey, USA

Profession: Mathematician and Physicist

Field: Representation theory, Harmonic analysis, Lie groups

Contributions

  • Made pioneering contributions to the representation theory of Lie groups and Lie algebras.
  • Known for the Harish-Chandra isomorphism and Harish-Chandra module.
  • His work connected quantum mechanics with abstract algebra through group theory.
  • Influenced the Langlands program and modern theoretical physics.

Awards and Recognition

  • Fellow of the Royal Society (FRS)
  • Member of the National Academy of Sciences (USA)
  • The Harish-Chandra Research Institute in India is named in his honor.

Legacy

  • Considered one of the greatest Indian-American mathematicians of the 20th century.
  • Laid the foundation for several areas of modern mathematics and mathematical physics.
  • His rigorous approach continues to influence research in abstract algebra and geometry.
